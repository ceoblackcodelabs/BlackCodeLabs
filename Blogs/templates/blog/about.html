Our Mission

Scholar Voice is the institutional blog where the administration shares official updates, announcements, and stories with the entire student body and wider community. We believe in transparency, timely communication, and celebrating the achievements of everyone on campus.

Every story we publish is vetted, accurate, and crafted to keep you informed — not just notified.

What you'll find here

  • Graduation and academic calendar updates
  • Scholarships and financial aid announcements
  • Campus life, events and sports coverage
  • Important notices from the administration
  • Student success stories and alumni spotlights
  • Research highlights and faculty achievements

What we stand for

Our story

How Scholar Voice grew from a notice board into a campus institution

2018
Founded as a static notice board

A small team of administrators launched Scholar Voice as a simple HTML page to replace scattered email chains and physical notice boards.

2019
First student contributors join

We opened the platform to student journalists, tripling our output and bringing new energy and perspective to campus coverage.

2021
Full redesign & comment system launched

A complete rebuild introduced categories, tags, search, and the comment system that turned Scholar Voice into a genuine conversation space.

2023
10,000 registered readers milestone

Scholar Voice crossed 10 k registered accounts — proof that the campus community wanted more than announcements; they wanted stories.

Today
Still growing, still telling your stories

We publish multiple times a week, have contributors from every faculty, and are constantly building new ways for the community to engage.

Common questions

Quick answers about Scholar Voice

Is it free to read Scholar Voice?
Yes, completely. All articles are free to read without creating an account. You only need an account if you want to comment, like posts, or save your preferences.
How do I submit a story or tip?
Use our contact form to send story ideas or tips. You can attach an image to give us more context. Our editorial team reviews every submission.
Can I become a contributing writer?
Absolutely. We welcome student and faculty contributors. Send us a brief pitch through the contact form and the editor-in-chief will get back to you within a few days.
How often do you publish?
We publish new articles multiple times per week. Breaking news and urgent announcements go up same-day; longer features and profiles run on a weekly schedule.
How do I report an inaccuracy?
Accuracy matters to us. If you spot an error, please use the contact form and include the article title and the correction. We'll review it promptly and issue a correction notice on the article.
