The Moon Card & What Your Subconscious Is Trying to Tell You

♕
The Empress Jasmin
Spiritual Guide · Tarot Reader · Life Coach
Published June 12, 2025
Read Time 8 min read
Category Tarot Wisdom

When the Moon card appears in a reading, most people feel a flicker of unease. Its imagery — the howling wolves, the crayfish emerging from deep water, the twin pillars standing in silent guard — can feel unsettling at first glance. But the Moon is not a card of darkness. It is a card of revelation.

It is the universe holding up a lantern to the parts of yourself you have been too afraid — or too busy — to look at.

In over a decade of tarot readings and spiritual guidance, I have seen the Moon card show up at the most pivotal moments of my clients' lives — not as a warning, but as an invitation. An invitation to stop pretending. To stop performing. To finally see what is really there beneath the surface of your carefully constructed life.

"The Moon does not create the shadows. It simply illuminates what was always already there — waiting for you to be ready to see it."

What the Moon Card Actually Means

The Moon sits at position XVIII in the Major Arcana — and in numerology, 18 reduces to 9, the number of completion, wisdom, and the turning of cycles. This alone tells you something important: the Moon is not the beginning of a journey into the unknown. It is the final threshold before clarity arrives.

Traditional interpretations of the Moon include illusion, confusion, anxiety, the subconscious, and hidden truths. But in my experience, these labels miss the deeper point. The Moon is not about being confused. It is about the courage it takes to sit with uncertainty long enough for the truth to reveal itself.

The creatures in the card — the crayfish, the wolf, the dog — represent three aspects of our inner world:

  • The crayfish is the deep subconscious — the primal, instinctive self rising from the waters of the unconscious mind, carrying what we have suppressed.
  • The wolf is our wild, untamed nature — the part of us that howls at what we cannot control, that fears the darkness of the unknown.
  • The dog is our domesticated self — the version of us shaped by societal expectations, relationships, and the roles we have been taught to play.

All three stand beneath the same moon. All three are you.

✦

Why Your Subconscious Sends This Card

In my practice, the Moon card rarely arrives out of nowhere. It almost always appears when something has been building beneath the surface — a feeling you have been pushing down, a truth you have been avoiding, a pattern you have been repeating without fully understanding why.

Your subconscious is extraordinarily patient. It will whisper to you first — through dreams, through that persistent low-level anxiety, through the same emotional trigger showing up in different relationships, different jobs, different chapters of your life. It is only when the whisper goes unheard for long enough that it escalates.

The Moon card is that escalation. It is your subconscious saying, clearly and firmly: we need to look at this now.

— From a private reading session, 2024

The beautiful thing is that the subconscious is never trying to destabilize you. It is always, without exception, trying to protect you. It surfaces what is hidden because what is hidden is the very thing standing between you and the life — the love, the peace, the purpose — that you are meant to be living.

Three Questions to Ask When the Moon Appears

1. What am I pretending not to know?

This is almost always the first and most important question. There is typically something in your life — a relationship, a situation, a choice — where you already sense the truth but are not quite ready to fully own it. The Moon is permission to own it.

2. What do I feel in my body that I have been ignoring?

The body is an extraordinary messenger. Anxiety without a clear source, recurring tension in specific parts of your body, exhaustion that sleep does not cure — these are your subconscious speaking through flesh and nerve and bone. When the Moon appears, pay close attention to what your body is already telling you.

3. What story am I telling myself that might not be true?

The Moon governs illusion — and not all illusions are external. Some of the most powerful illusions we live inside are the ones we have built ourselves, brick by careful brick, to make sense of things that happened to us before we had the tools to understand them. Childhood stories. Old wounds. Beliefs about what we deserve, what love looks like, what is possible for us.

The Moon invites you to examine these stories. Not to destroy yourself with them — but to see them clearly, and then to ask: is this still true? Does this still serve me?

"You are not your patterns. You are not your past. You are the awareness that can see them — and the power that can choose differently."

✦

Working With the Moon's Energy

When the Moon appears in your reading, here is what I recommend to the souls I work with:

  • Slow down. This is not a card that rewards urgency. The Moon asks you to move with the rhythm of the tides — steadily, patiently, trusting that clarity is coming even when you cannot yet see it.
  • Journal. Write without editing. Write what you feel, not what you think you should feel. The subconscious speaks most freely when the critical mind is not curating every word.
  • Pay attention to your dreams. The liminal space between sleep and waking is where the Moon's wisdom is most accessible. Keep a notebook by your bed. Even fragments of dreams carry meaning.
  • Resist the urge to force resolution. The Moon's cycle teaches us that after darkness comes the new moon — a clean slate. What needs to emerge will emerge, in its own time, if you create the conditions for it to do so safely.
  • Trust your intuition above all else. During a Moon period, your logical mind may feel foggy and unreliable. This is not a malfunction. This is the universe directing your attention away from the head and toward the deeper knowing that lives in your gut, your heart, your bones.
✦

A Final Word on the Moon

The Moon card used to unsettle me too — back in the early years of my practice, when I was still learning to trust what I could not fully see. I wanted certainty. I wanted maps and answers and clear instructions.

What the Moon taught me — and what I now carry into every reading I give — is that the most profound clarity does not arrive through force. It arrives through presence. Through the willingness to sit in the beautiful, terrifying, sacred uncertainty of not yet knowing — and to trust that the light is coming.

It always does.

If the Moon card has appeared in your life recently and you feel called to explore what your subconscious is trying to show you, I would be honoured to sit with you in that space. A reading is not about predicting your future — it is about helping you see yourself, and your life, more clearly.

That clarity changes everything.
