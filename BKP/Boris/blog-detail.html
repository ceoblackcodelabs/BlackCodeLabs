Nairobi Property Prices: 2026 Mid-Year Outlook

👤 By Susan Wairimu 📅 June 10, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read
📰

Halfway through 2026, Nairobi's real estate market continues to show resilience despite global economic headwinds. Across our tracked neighborhoods — Karen, Westlands, Kilimani, Kileleshwa, Riverside, and Runda — average asking prices have moved modestly upward, driven by a combination of constrained new supply and steady demand from both local buyers and the diaspora.

Construction Costs Remain the Key Pressure Point

Developers continue to cite the cost of imported finishes and steel as the single largest driver of new-build pricing. While cement prices have stabilized somewhat compared to last year, the shilling's performance against major currencies still adds uncertainty to projects relying on imported materials.

"Buyers in 2026 are more price-sensitive but better informed than ever — they're doing their own market research before even calling an agent."

Infrastructure Is Reshaping Demand

Completed and ongoing road projects, particularly along the Nairobi Expressway corridor and the Northern Bypass, have made previously peripheral areas significantly more attractive. We're seeing this reflected in land values along these routes, with some plots appreciating faster than established neighborhoods.

What This Means for Buyers and Sellers

For buyers, this remains a market where patience and proper due diligence pay off — particularly around title verification and understanding true market value rather than asking price. For sellers, well-presented properties in established neighborhoods continue to move quickly when priced realistically against comparable recent sales.

We'll continue tracking these trends through the second half of the year and will share an updated outlook in our quarterly market report.
